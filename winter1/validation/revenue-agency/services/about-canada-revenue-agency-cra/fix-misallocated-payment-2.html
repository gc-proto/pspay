For which of these do you need a payment arrangement?

  • personal taxes
  • a business
  • a government service unrelated to taxes (such as EI or student loans)

Personal payment arrangement

A payment arrangement allows you to make smaller payments over time until you have paid your entire debt including applicable interest.

Find out if you qualify

To help calculate payment arrangement options before speaking to the CRA, you can use the:

  • Payment Arrangement Calculator to calculate payment options with the prescribed CRA interest rates
  • Income and Expense Worksheet to help you to calculate your available net income to pay your debt

Before you make a payment arrangement, you may need to show that you have tried to pay your debt in full by borrowing money or reducing your expenses.

Business payment arrangement

A payment arrangement is an agreement that your business makes with the CRA. It allows your business to make smaller payments over time until you have paid your entire debt including applicable interest.

Find out if you qualify

To help calculate payment arrangement options before speaking to the CRA, you can use the:

  • Payment Arrangement Calculator to calculate payment options with the prescribed CRA interest rates
  • Income and Expense Worksheet to help you to calculate your available net income to pay your debt

Before your business makes a payment arrangement, you may need to show that you have tried to pay your debt in full by borrowing money or reducing expenses. As a business, you may also have to fully disclose your financial records.

Once you have made a payment arrangement your business must also:

  • maintain a regular payment plan
  • always file all returns on time
  • stay up to date with tax obligations

If you fail to do any of these things, we may cancel the payment arrangement and take action to recover the debt.

Online:

In most cases, you can make the arrangement on your own without speaking with the CRA. You can set up a payment arrangement with pre-authorized debit through My Business Account.

Payment arrangement for other government services

The CRA collects amounts owed for other government programs:

  • defaulted Canada Student Loans
  • Employment Insurance overpayments and penalties
  • Training Allowances Payment System overpayments
  • Canada Pension Plan overpayments
  • grants and contributions overpayments
  • operations and maintenance receivables
  • other Employment and Social Development Canada program overpayments and/or penalties

If you cannot pay your other government programs debt in full now, you may qualify for a payment arrangement or for help under the financial hardship provisions.

Declaring bankruptcy will not release you from all types of debt

If you feel you are insolvent or are considering bankruptcy, a bankruptcy discharge could release you from the obligation to repay debts you had when you filed for bankruptcy.

Debt that will not be erased by bankruptcy

Student loans: Bankruptcy will not release you from debt on a student loan, unless 7 years have passed since you finished your studies.

owed on GST/HST, payroll deductions and other amounts held in trust. Unless 7 years after you completed your studies, bankruptcy will not release you from student loan debt.

GST/HST, payroll and amounts held in trust: Bankruptcy might not release you from debt owed on GST/HST, payroll deductions and other amounts held in trust. See what other debt is considered to be held in trust
